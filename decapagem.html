Decapagem

the collapse of the industrial body

The research team consisted in five diverse artists from different countries, who were selected from previous experience collaborating in areas such as dance, performance, poetry, music and visual arts.

In the course of the residency the artists operated on a spontaneous free flow as a way of resisting what we ended up calling the „industrial body" - which is the body that operates according to numbers (hours, production, salary). Our research was presented as an immersive performance for twenty people.

These twenty witnesses, which had no information about what they were going take part in, were suggested to use protective equipment and enter an industrial space dedicated to a process of applying hot zinc to steel, which is normally neither open to the public nor to artists to experiment in. The audience was guided through fifty minutes of soft spoken poetry, industrial noises, local folclore, projected visuals and movement.

The witnesses described the immersion as „Violent meditation" and „Industrial healing".

Katharina Löffler was deeply involved in the research, development and presentation of this project.
